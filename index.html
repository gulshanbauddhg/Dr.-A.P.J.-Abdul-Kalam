Dr. A.P.J. Abdul Kalam

1931-2015

About Dr. A.P.J. Abdul Kalam

Avul Pakir Jainulabdeen Abdul Kalam was an Indian aerospace scientist and politician who served as the 11th President of India from 2002 to 2007. He was born on October 15, 1931, in Rameswaram, Tamil Nadu, India. Kalam played a pivotal role in India's civilian space program and military missile development, earning him the nickname "Missile Man of India."

Kalam studied physics and aerospace engineering and worked as a scientist at the Defence Research and Development Organisation (DRDO) and the Indian Space Research Organisation (ISRO). He was a key figure in India's Pokhran-II nuclear tests in 1998, which established India as a nuclear power.

In 2002, Kalam was elected as the President of India, where he was widely respected for his humility, vision, and dedication to education and youth development. After his presidency, he continued to inspire millions through his lectures and writings until his passing on July 27, 2015.

In India, Kalam is remembered as a people's president and a visionary leader who inspired the youth to dream big and work hard to achieve their goals.

Biography

  • Born: October 15, 1931, Rameswaram, Tamil Nadu, India
  • Died: July 27, 2015, Shillong, Meghalaya, India
  • Education: Madras Institute of Technology (MIT)
  • Occupation: Aerospace Scientist, Politician
  • Notable Works: Pokhran-II nuclear tests, President of India (2002-2007)

Famous Quote
